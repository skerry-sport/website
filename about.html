About the Association

Learn about our purpose, governance, and the boats we sail

Our Purpose

The Skerry Sport One-Design Class Association (SSODCA) exists to:

  • Promote sailing, racing, and fellowship among owners and sailors of boats constructed from the Skerry kit or plans as published by Chesapeake Light Craft (CLC)
  • Maintain the one-design integrity of the class through consistent rules and fair governance
  • Support local fleets, events, and sailors worldwide

The Skerry Sport

The Skerry Sport is a 15-foot, double-ended sailboat designed by John Harris at Chesapeake Light Craft. This beautiful boat features:

Design Features

  • Length: 15 feet overall
  • Beam: 4 feet 6 inches
  • Construction: Glued-lapstrake plywood
  • Minimum Weight: 95 pounds (dry)
  • Rig Options: Balanced Lug, Sprit, or Sloop

Built for Everyone

The Skerry Sport is designed to be accessible to home builders while providing excellent sailing performance. The glued-lapstrake construction method creates a strong, lightweight hull with beautiful traditional lines.

Annual General Meeting

The Association holds an Annual General Meeting (AGM), either in person or virtually, where:

  • Officers are elected by Full Members
  • Major decisions are ratified
  • Rule changes are discussed and voted upon
  • The future direction of the class is planned

Spirit of the Class

The Skerry Sport One-Design class is intended for sailors who value craftsmanship, accessibility, skill development, and community. While modest competition is encouraged, the emphasis remains on inclusivity and fellowship on the water.

"We believe that sailing skill should outweigh construction differences, and that the joy of sailing these beautiful boats should be accessible to builders and sailors of all experience levels."
