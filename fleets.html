What is a Fleet?

A Fleet is a group of three or more eligible Skerry Sport boats that organize locally for racing, events, and fellowship. Fleets serve as the primary point of contact for sailors in a geographic area and help organize local activities within the broader class association.

Learn How to Start a Fleet

Starting a New Fleet

Any group of three or more eligible boats may form a Fleet. Here's how to get started:

1

Find Fellow Sailors

Connect with other Skerry Sport owners or builders in your area. You need at least three boats to form a fleet. Check local sailing clubs, online forums, or CLC owner groups.

2

Elect a Fleet Captain

Choose someone to serve as your Fleet Captain. This person will be your primary contact with the class association and help organize local activities.

3

Register with the Association

Contact the class association to register your new fleet. Provide fleet name, location, Fleet Captain contact information, and list of founding boats.

4

Establish Local Policies

Develop any local policies or procedures you need, provided they don't contradict the class rules. Consider things like racing schedules, social events, and measurement procedures.

5

Start Sailing!

Begin organizing races, fun sails, and social events. Help grow your local Skerry Sport community!

Fleet Autonomy

Fleets have significant autonomy to organize themselves according to local needs and preferences:

  • Racing Formats: Choose course types, scoring systems, and series formats that work for your area
  • Local Policies: Establish additional local policies (e.g., crew weight rules, handicaps) as long as they don't contradict class rules
  • Event Schedule: Set your own racing and social event calendar
  • Meeting Frequency: Determine how often your fleet meets and communicates

Support for New Fleets

The class association is committed to supporting new fleets:

  • Guidance on organizing and running a fleet
  • Template documents for fleet policies and procedures
  • Connection with other fleets for sharing best practices
  • Technical support for measurement and rule interpretation
  • Promotion of your fleet activities to attract new members
