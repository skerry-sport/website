Class Rules

Technical specifications and racing rules for the Skerry Sport One-Design Class

Note: These are the core class rules. For complete details, please refer to our founding documents repository.

1. Purpose and Objectives

The Skerry Sport One-Design Class exists to:

  • Promote safe, friendly, and enjoyable racing in boats built from CLC Skerry kits or plans as published by Chesapeake Light Craft (CLC)
  • Provide fair competition where sailing skill outweighs construction differences
  • Support local fleets with consistent rules while allowing for builder creativity within reason
  • Foster community among Skerry Sport sailors through organized events and good sportsmanship

2. Boat Eligibility and Measurement

2.1 Eligibility

Boats eligible for competition must be substantially similar to the CLC Skerry design as published by CLC. Variations due to home-building are anticipated, but they must not materially affect performance.

2.2 Registration

Each racing boat must register with its fleet and the Association, declaring rig type and providing a record of construction details (materials, modifications, weight corrections, etc.).

2.3 Measurement Certificates

Boats that have passed measurement will receive a certificate of compliance valid until altered by repair or modification.

3. Technical Specifications

3.1 Hull and Structure

Specification Requirement Notes
Length Overall 15'0" Within builder tolerance
Beam 4'6" Within builder tolerance
Construction Glued-lapstrake plywood Double-ended, per published design
Minimum Weight 95 lbs (dry) Corrector weights may be required
Flotation Required Standard air tanks or equivalent foam

3.2 Rig and Sails

  • Supported Rigs: Balanced Lug, Sprit, and Sloop rigs as published in the official CLC Skerry plans
  • Racing Divisions: Fleets may race rigs separately or together under a handicap system
  • Sail Requirements: Within rig divisions, sails must be substantially similar in cut, material, and area
  • Spars: Must be of wood construction and built to plan dimensions

3.3 Foils

  • Daggerboard and rudder must conform to the plan's shape, dimensions, and placement
  • Construction may use appropriate marine wood materials
  • Modifications for performance gain are not allowed

3.4 Equipment

  • Running rigging may be upgraded for safety and durability, with fleet approval
  • Hiking straps or other hiking aids are prohibited
  • Crewing arrangements are flexible; fleets may regulate crew weight

4. Racing Rules

4.1 Rules of Competition

Races will be conducted under the World Sailing Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS), except as specifically modified by this document or local sailing instructions.

4.2 Divisions

Local fleets may organize separate divisions for each rig type or race them together under a mutually agreed handicap.

4.3 Safety Requirements

  • All sailors must wear USCG-approved personal flotation devices when racing
  • Boats must carry any additional safety gear required by host clubs or event organizers

4.4 Sportsmanship

Competitors are expected to assist other sailors in distress, show respect and courtesy, and prioritize safety before competition.

5. Rule Changes and Amendments

  • Amendments may be proposed by any member of the Association
  • Proposed changes must be submitted in writing to the Secretary
  • Amendments require approval by a majority of voting members
  • Technical rule changes must have the recommendation of the Commodore and Fleet Captain before being put to a vote

6. Spirit of the Class

The Skerry Sport One-Design class is intended for sailors who value craftsmanship, accessibility, skill development, and community. While modest competition is encouraged, the emphasis remains on inclusivity and fellowship on the water.
